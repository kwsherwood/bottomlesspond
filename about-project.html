Bottomless Pond: or, the Walden Zone

Bottomless Pond is a digital composition with relation to e-poetry and net art. It aims to engage users/readers/visitors in a thoughtful, reflective, immersive (?) & slow reading of excerpted sentences from the Thoreau corpus. A soothing, slow-text environment creates an experience differentiating from much online reading which is characterized by skipping, linking, and the rapid eye saccades of distracted thought. In this sense, normative online reading practices might be symptomatic of the emptying of language that concerned Thoreau; hence the interest in returning to a different kind of reading, through an environment and interface that offers language in an environment of near stillness. (Or as near as a networked digital composition might get.)

Source: Thoreau's Walden or Life in the Woods provides a rich reservoir of reflections on time, technology, the self and mind in a changing environment. As a kind of resistance text it advocates a kind of slow living and a reading of classics. Now, itself, as classic, Walden speaks to the contemporary reader in an increasingly distracted era. Thus creating a remediation(remix, adaptation, ?) Allows for a slowed reading experience that detourns the digital ethos of the Bottomless Pond platform.

References: Walden, Buddhism, ambient poetics, kinetic poem, anti-Futurism

Interface: The text appears slowly, a sentence or phrase - fading up or emerging from digital shadows - giving the reader an indicator of temporality, but of a slow unfolding. It sets a pace or rhythm that is on the biological scale of attentiveness and not the flicker of the screen (despite, obviously and ironically being presented through that very medium).

Text: Selected, arranged, and recontextualized (but NOT altered) the Thoreau text is chosen to engage attention but also to allow some free wandering of the mind. The reader might feel an arousal of attention but not like he is receiving a lecture. Hence sentences at most are revealed. The day dreaming of a poetic-philosophical attentiveness is invited by the pairing of sentence, perhaps with a moderate disjunction and temporal and/or spatial gap. One line .... revealing ... and then the second part of the couplet. These must have some plausible associative, thematic, phonic, or lexical connection. We don't want the reader to perceive it as nonsense but as almost sense - couplet as Koan?

Database: The piece should seem to pour slowly forth as from a bottomless well, disclosed over time (minutes for the resolution of a verse; perhaps hours, days, weeks, a year for the full. For example, it might disclose a pair of lines over a five minute period and then be refreshed with a new set, perhaps appearing one at a time.
